Popunder and Interstitial are both full-page formats. Both demand attention. Both generate strong CPMs. But they work in fundamentally different ways, and choosing between them — or running both — depends on what you're optimizing for.

## How they differ

**Popunder** opens a full-page ad in a background tab, triggered by a user click anywhere on the page. The visitor doesn't see it immediately — they find it when they close or switch tabs. The active browsing session is never interrupted.

**Interstitial** takes over the screen between page views. The visitor sees it right now, with a close button that appears after a short delay. There's no ignoring it.

The core trade-off: Popunder is invisible during the session but found later. Interstitial is impossible to miss but interrupts the flow.

## UX cost

Popunder has a low perceived UX cost because it doesn't disrupt what the visitor is doing. The downside: some users never find the background tab, or they close it reflexively. The ad loads, but engagement may be lower.

Interstitial has a higher UX cost. It pauses navigation. Done well — fired on the second or third pageview, with a clear close button — visitors accept it. Done poorly — fired immediately, close button hidden — it drives bounce.

Neither format is inherently "bad for UX." Both depend on implementation, frequency capping, and timing.

## Performance ranges

Direct comparisons are tricky because performance varies wildly by geo, vertical, and traffic quality. That said, some general patterns hold:

**Popunder** tends to deliver higher raw impression volume at lower CPMs. Advertisers running broad offers — utilities, sweepstakes, gaming — often prefer it because the format works like a landing page: you're promoting a full URL with no creative constraints.

**Interstitial** typically generates higher CTR per impression because the ad occupies the full screen and the visitor must interact with it (even if that interaction is closing it). CPMs tend to run higher than Popunder for the same traffic. It's a strong choice for app installs, video-first campaigns, and offers where visual impact matters.

## Which verticals prefer which

- **Utilities and VPN:** Popunder wins. The landing page format is ideal.
- **Gaming and app installs:** Interstitial wins. The full-screen visual drives installs.
- **Sweepstakes:** Both work. Test and compare.
- **E-commerce:** Interstitial has an edge because product images benefit from screen real estate.
- **Content arbitrage:** Popunder works well because the hidden tab means the source page retains the visitor.

## Running both without stacking

You can run Popunder and Interstitial on the same site — but never on the same pageview. Stacking two full-page formats creates a terrible experience: the visitor hits an interstitial and then discovers a popunder tab behind their browser. That's a fast path to complaints and lower retention.

The rule is simple: if a pageview triggers a Popunder, it should not also trigger an Interstitial. Most ad platforms, aiaiaiads included, let you set frequency and format exclusion rules at the placement level.

A practical setup for publishers:

- **Page 1 (entry):** No full-page format. Let the visitor land cleanly.
- **Page 2:** Interstitial between page 1 and page 2.
- **Page 3+:** Popunder on next click, capped at once per 24 hours.

This way, your most engaged visitors see both formats across their session, but never stacked. Your eCPM benefits from format diversity, and your audience doesn't suffer.

## The bottom line

Popunder and Interstitial aren't rivals — they're complements. Popunder gives you reach and volume. Interstitial gives you attention and higher per-impression value. Test both, read the data, and use frequency rules to keep them from stepping on each other.
